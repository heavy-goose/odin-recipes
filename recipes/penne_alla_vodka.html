Penne Alla Vodka

Description

Today we learn how to make penne vodka aka penne alla vodka, aka penne with vodka sauce. Penne alla vodka is one of the most delicious pasta even and it reminds me of childhood. I set out to recreate that ideal version of penne vodka that I remember as a child.

Ingridients

  • 1 LB Penne
  • 1/4 LB Prosciutto (optional)
  • 3 Cloves Garlic, Sliced Thin
  • 3/4 Cup Cream
  • 3-4 Tablespoons Vodka
  • 1 Can Whole Peeled Tomatoes
  • 1 Teaspoon Red Pepper Flakes
  • 1 Stem of Basil
  • 1/4 Cup Parsley, finely diced for garnish
  • 1/2 Cup Parmesan for garnish
  • 2-3 Tablespoons Olive Oil
  • 2 Tablespoons Unsalted Butter
  • Salt & Pepper

Steps

  1. To start, prepare the garlic, tomatoes, and prosciutto. Slice up the garlic thin. Then slice the prosciutto into little strips. If you want this to be meatless, then omit the prosciutto and begin the sauce just like you would with weekday sauce. Puree your tomatoes in a food mill or a blender.
  2. Get a pot of salted water on the stove for the pasta and a wide-bottomed pan next to it to cook the sauce.
  3. In the pan, add the prosciutto and a few tablespoons of olive oil and cook over medium-high heat until the prosciutto just begins to crisp up. Then add the basil and garlic. The key is to try and time it so that you add the garlic at the perfect time to allow for it to cook and not burn, and for the prosciutto to get crisp. If you don’t nail it, that’s fine, but when you start to see the garlic browning too much, it's time to deglaze and stop the cooking. (If you want to omit the prosciutto, then just use extra olive oil and start this sauce by adding garlic and basil to the oil and then adding the vodka and then the sauce.)
  4. Turn the heat off for a minute and then add the vodka. Let it sizzle and calm down. Turn the heat back on and reduce the vodka till it’s almost nothing. Now add the tomatoes. Cook the tomatoes on medium heat until they reduce and become very thick. You can tell how thick it's getting by dragging a flat wooden spoon across the pan and observing how the sauce holds itself. Once you’ve cooked it down till most of the water is gone and you can see the bottom of the pan when you mix the sauce, it’s time to add the cream. Bring the sauce to a simmer and cook for a few minutes to thicken.
  5. Cook the penne in salty water. If you want extra sauce, reduce two cans of tomatoes and use a little more vodka and cream. I like a lot of sauce for this pasta, so I recommend making extra, but this recipe will satisfy 1 lb of pasta with just enough sauce.
  6. Once the pasta is al dente, drain and transfer to the sauce. Marry the sauce with the pasta for a couple of minutes until the sauce coats the pasta. Drop in 1-2 tablespoons of cold butter and a little pasta water to control the consistency of the sauce. Serve immediately with Parmesan and fresh parsley.
